About Me

I'm an AI/ML researcher and engineer with a strong focus on embodied AI, natural language processing, and building intelligent agents. My work lies at the intersection of language models, robotics, and autonomous systems, where I develop systems that can perceive, reason, and act in complex environments.

Currently, I'm working on uncertainty quantification in autonomous driving systems and developing novel approaches to enhance the reliability of vision-language models for real-world applications. My research explores how we can make AI systems more robust, interpretable, and capable of handling the complexities of physical environments.

I have hands-on experience building end-to-end AI systems, from designing data pipelines and training large language models to deploying scalable ML infrastructure. My work spans across both academic research and industry applications, with a particular interest in creating AI agents that can effectively interact with and learn from their environment.
